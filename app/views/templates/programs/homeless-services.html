Homeless Services

Providing essential support and resources to individuals experiencing homelessness in Kenosha County.

Kenosha Welcome Center (KWC) offers vital support to individuals experiencing homelessness. Our services are designed to address immediate needs while connecting guests to long-term resources. From survival supplies to referrals, we are here to help our community members regain stability.

Coordinated Entry

What is Coordinated Entry?

Coordinated Entry is a streamlined system that ensures individuals experiencing homelessness are connected to the most appropriate housing and support services available. KWC acts as an access point, helping guests navigate local resources.

How KWC Helps:

  • We conduct initial assessments to determine eligibility for local shelters and housing programs.
  • We guide guests through the referral process to ensure a seamless transition into appropriate services.
  • Partnerships with agencies like KHDS and other community organizations strengthen our ability to serve.

Survival Items

We provide essential items to help individuals face their immediate challenges:

  • Sleeping bags
  • Emergency blankets
  • Tents and tarps (based on availability)
  • Personal hygiene kits
  • Plastic Bags to keep items dry
Supplies are limited and distributed on a first-come, first-served basis.

Cold Weather Items

Winter in Wisconsin can be life-threatening for individuals experiencing homelessness. KWC ensures access to:

  • Hats and gloves
  • Hand warmers
  • Thermal underwear
  • Insulated jackets

3-Day Survival Food Packs

Pack Contents

  • Pop-top canned goods
  • Single-serve snacks
  • Bottled water
  • Eating utensils

No Equipment Needed

  • No Stove Required
  • No Microwave Required
  • No Fridge required
